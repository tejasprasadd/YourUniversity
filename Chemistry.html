Bachelor of Science in Chemistry

Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Chemistry is a four-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on the study of chemical elements and compounds, their properties, reactions, and interactions with other substances. The program aims to provide students with a strong foundation in chemistry and related disciplines, including physics, mathematics, and biology.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the Bachelor of Science in Chemistry program can pursue careers in various fields, including:

  • Pharmaceuticals and biotechnology

  • Petrochemicals and energy

  • Materials science and nanotechnology

  • Environmental science and sustainability

  • Food and agriculture

  • Forensics and law enforcement

Course Requirements

To earn a Bachelor of Science in Chemistry, students typically need to complete the following core courses:

  • General Chemistry I and II

  • Organic Chemistry I and II

  • Physical Chemistry I and II

  • Analytical Chemistry

  • Biochemistry

  • Inorganic Chemistry

  • Mathematics for Science and Engineering

  • Physics for Scientists and Engineers

  • Chemical Safety and Laboratory Techniques

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements for Bachelor of Science in Chemistry programs vary depending on the institution. However, most programs typically require applicants to have:

  • A high school diploma or equivalent

  • Strong grades in science and math courses

  • Standardized test scores (e.g., SAT or ACT)

  • Letters of recommendation

  • A personal statement or essay
